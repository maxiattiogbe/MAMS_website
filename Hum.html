Humanities

This class is taught by Ms. Small. It is simultaneously and English class and a History class. So far we have covered a vast timespan of human history from the time when humans societies began to form up until the Los Angeles race riots. We covered a variety of texts and analyzed them not only for their historical meaning, but also aspects you would expect to analyze in a traditional English class such as rhetoric. In class discussions are an especially useful component of this class not only for better comprehension of texts and exposure to new perspectives, but also for ideas for essays.

The book incoming juniors must read for the assigned summer reading for Humanities is Walden Pond by Henry David Thoreau. We had to write a journal for most of the chapters as the summer assignment. In the first weeks of school, we discussed the book and started working in assigned groups on short about five to ten minute plays or skits related to the book in some way. My group decided to make a skit exploring what would happen if Thoreau found himself in modern times. See the pdf below for the full script.
