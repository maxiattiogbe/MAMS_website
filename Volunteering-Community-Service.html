Community Service

This summer, I volunteered as part of the Easterseals Massachusetts Adaptive and Accessible Swim Program. It has held at Bennett Field Pool on Friday mornings for six weeks from June 28th to August 2nd. Each day the program, there were two sessions of about 45 minutes each. The first session was for kids ages 5-12, while the second was for children 13 years old or older and adults. I, along with 3 to 4 other volunteers around my age, along with some adults helped the instructor set up and clean up after the program. Additionally, I, usually with another volunteer, was assigned to greet the participants as they came in and take attendance each week. Although the program is not meant to teach participants how to swim, most of them showed improvement on their abilities to safely move through water. I have been swimming for several years know, so I was glad to use the skills I had attained to help teach others how to swim. I enjoy swimming; this program has helped me spread that enjoyment to others. That made this a meaningful experience for me.

I also have been volunteering for the Easterseals Massachusetts Accessible Martial Arts Program. The goal of this program is to help people with disabilities learn self defense techniques. I volunteer when it is held at the gym of the YWCA at 1 Salem Street, Worcester MA. It is now also held at the Non-Profit Center at 89 South Street in Boston, Massachusetts. It is a lot of fun to help people with disabilities learn a variety of self-defense techniques such as different types of strikes and blocks. It is also a good source of exercise.
